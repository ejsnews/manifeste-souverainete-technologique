## Reading the Table

### Columns
**Sovereignty**: Geopolitical risk linked to import dependence. 🔴 means critical dependence on global markets (OPEC, Russia, China for materials). 🟢 means entirely local production from non-conflictual residual resources.

**Non-Carbonized**: The source does not produce CO₂ during operation. ⚠️ means partial neutrality or a short but non-zero cycle.

**Decarbonizes**: The source goes beyond carbon neutrality — it actively sequesters CO₂ from the atmosphere. Only Haffner Energy achieves this thanks to biochar (a solid and permanent carbon sink).

**Air Pol.**: Emissions of fine particles, NOx, SOx during production. Coal and oil are the most harmful to public health — their real cost, including respiratory and cardiovascular diseases, far exceeds their face value.

**Soil Pol.**: Impact on land (mining, waste storage, leakage risks). "Regenerates" for Haffner means that biochar actively improves soil structure and fertility.

**Price (€/kWh)**: Production cost, excluding subsidies, in the French 2026 context. For Haffner H6, the "net + biochar" line includes co-product credits (200 kg of biochar at €275–420/t per tonne of biomass processed).

**CAPEX (€/kW)**: Initial investment per installed kilowatt. The new EPR2 nuclear reactor (€9,000/kW) illustrates the French paradox: the most expensive technology to build, with the longest lead times (15–20 years), for a centralized result that is not sovereign on materials (imported uranium).
