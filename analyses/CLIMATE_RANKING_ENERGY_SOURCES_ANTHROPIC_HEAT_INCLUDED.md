### Methodology — 6 criteria × 4 points max = 24 pts

| Criterion              | Weighting | Description |
|------------------------|-----------|-----------|
| 🏭 **Sovereignty**     | /4        | Geopolitical dependence on imports |
| ♻️ **Non-CO₂**         | /4        | Zero CO₂ emissions in operation |
| 🌱 **Decarbonizes**    | /4        | Actively sequesters existing atmospheric CO₂ |
| 💨 **Air**             | /4        | Zero fine particles, NOx, SOx |
| 🌍 **Soils**           | /4        | Zero or positive impact on soils and water |
| 🌡️ **Net Heat**        | /4        | Zero anthropic heat added to the atmosphere |

> **Note on anthropic heat:** Any stock energy (fossil, nuclear) adds net heat to the Earth's envelope, independently of CO₂.  
> Flow energies (solar, wind, hydropower) intercept energy already present in the natural cycle — they do not add net heat.  
> Haffner Energy is the only system that **reduces** dissipated heat by sequestering the carbon that was blocking its evacuation into space.

### What This Ranking Reveals

Conventional renewable energies top out at **15-18/24** — they do not decarbonize; they only limit new emissions.

**Nuclear drops to 12/24** despite its excellence on CO₂: the fatal heat dissipated into rivers (2 GW thermal for 1 GW electric across a fleet of 56 reactors) and radioactive waste with no permanent solution in France heavily penalize the score.

**Haffner H6 exceeds the 24-point maximum** — the only system with a net positive effect on the climate, which sequesters existing carbon instead of simply avoiding new emissions.

**The H4/S-iC**, currently under development, aims for the same level with significantly higher efficiency and a substantially lower unit cost — data not yet disclosed to the market.
